# The commutant of the symmetric Toeplitz operator system

Let \(\mathcal T_N^{\mathrm{sym}}\subseteq M_N(\mathbb C)\) denote the vector space of symmetric Toeplitz matrices. Let \(J\) be the reversal matrix,
\[
J e_j=e_{N+1-j}.
\]
For \(0\le k\le N-1\), let \(H_k\) be the symmetric Toeplitz basis matrix
\[
(H_k)_{ij}=\mathbf 1_{\{|i-j|=k\}}.
\]
Thus \(H_0=I\), \(H_1\) is the adjacency matrix of the path graph on \(N\) vertices, and
\[
H_{N-1}=e_1e_N^*+e_Ne_1^*.
\]

## Theorem

For every \(N\ge2\),
\[
\bigl(\mathcal T_N^{\mathrm{sym}}\bigr)'
=
\operatorname{span}_{\mathbb C}\{I,J\}.
\]
In fact, it is enough to commute with the two matrices \(H_1\) and \(H_{N-1}\):
\[
\{H_1,H_{N-1}\}'
=
\operatorname{span}_{\mathbb C}\{I,J\}.
\]

## Proof

The inclusion
\[
\operatorname{span}\{I,J\}
\subseteq
\bigl(\mathcal T_N^{\mathrm{sym}}\bigr)'
\]
is immediate. The identity commutes with everything, and every symmetric Toeplitz matrix is persymmetric, hence commutes with reversal \(J\).

It remains to prove the reverse inclusion.

Let \(X\in M_N(\mathbb C)\) commute with every symmetric Toeplitz matrix. In particular,
\[
[X,H_1]=0,
\qquad
[X,H_{N-1}]=0.
\]

### Step 1: diagonalize the path adjacency matrix

The matrix \(H_1\) has the orthonormal eigenvectors
\[
v_m(j)
=
\sqrt{\frac{2}{N+1}}
\sin\!\left(\frac{jm\pi}{N+1}\right),
\qquad
1\le j,m\le N,
\]
with eigenvalues
\[
\lambda_m
=
2\cos\!\left(\frac{m\pi}{N+1}\right).
\]
These eigenvalues are pairwise distinct. Therefore any matrix commuting with \(H_1\) is diagonal in the basis \(v_1,\dots,v_N\). Thus there exist scalars \(x_1,\dots,x_N\) such that
\[
Xv_m=x_m v_m.
\]

Equivalently, if \(U\) is the discrete sine transform matrix whose columns are the \(v_m\), then
\[
U^*XU=\operatorname{diag}(x_1,\dots,x_N).
\]

### Step 2: compute the endpoint coupling in the sine basis

The endpoint values satisfy
\[
v_m(N)
=
\sqrt{\frac{2}{N+1}}
\sin\!\left(\frac{Nm\pi}{N+1}\right)
=
(-1)^{m+1}v_m(1).
\]
Also,
\[
v_m(1)
=
\sqrt{\frac{2}{N+1}}
\sin\!\left(\frac{m\pi}{N+1}\right)
\ne0.
\]

Since
\[
H_{N-1}=e_1e_N^*+e_Ne_1^*,
\]
its matrix entries in the sine basis are
\[
\langle v_m,H_{N-1}v_\ell\rangle
=
v_m(1)v_\ell(N)+v_m(N)v_\ell(1).
\]
Using the endpoint relation gives
\[
\langle v_m,H_{N-1}v_\ell\rangle
=
v_m(1)v_\ell(1)
\left((-1)^{\ell+1}+(-1)^{m+1}\right).
\]
Therefore
\[
\langle v_m,H_{N-1}v_\ell\rangle
\ne0
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
m\equiv \ell\pmod 2.
\]
Indeed, when \(m\) and \(\ell\) have the same parity, the parenthesis equals \(\pm2\), and both endpoint factors are nonzero. When they have opposite parity, the parenthesis vanishes.

### Step 3: use the commutator with \(H_{N-1}\)

In the sine basis, \(X\) is diagonal. Hence
\[
\langle v_m,[X,H_{N-1}]v_\ell\rangle
=
(x_m-x_\ell)
\langle v_m,H_{N-1}v_\ell\rangle.
\]
Because \([X,H_{N-1}]=0\), we obtain
\[
(x_m-x_\ell)
\langle v_m,H_{N-1}v_\ell\rangle=0.
\]
Whenever \(m\equiv\ell\pmod2\), the second factor is nonzero. Therefore
\[
x_m=x_\ell
\qquad
\text{whenever }m\equiv\ell\pmod2.
\]
Thus there are scalars \(a_+\) and \(a_-\) such that
\[
x_m=
\begin{cases}
 a_+,&m\text{ odd},\\
 a_-,&m\text{ even}.
\end{cases}
\]

### Step 4: identify the two parity projectors

Reflection acts on the sine eigenvectors by
\[
(Jv_m)(j)
=v_m(N+1-j)
=(-1)^{m+1}v_m(j).
\]
Hence
\[
Jv_m=
\begin{cases}
+v_m,&m\text{ odd},\\
-v_m,&m\text{ even}.
\end{cases}
\]
Therefore \(X\) acts by \(a_+\) on the \(+1\) eigenspace of \(J\) and by \(a_-\) on the \(-1\) eigenspace of \(J\). Consequently,
\[
X
=
a_+\frac{I+J}{2}
+
a_-\frac{I-J}{2}.
\]
Equivalently,
\[
X
=
\frac{a_++a_-}{2}I
+
\frac{a_+-a_-}{2}J.
\]
Thus
\[
X\in\operatorname{span}_{\mathbb C}\{I,J\}.
\]
This proves
\[
\bigl(\mathcal T_N^{\mathrm{sym}}\bigr)'
=
\operatorname{span}_{\mathbb C}\{I,J\}.
\qquad\square
\]

## Corollary: full parity-preserving generated algebra

Let \(\operatorname{alg}^*(\mathcal T_N^{\mathrm{sym}})\) denote the unital \(*\)-algebra generated by the symmetric Toeplitz operator system. Since we are in finite dimension, the bicommutant theorem gives
\[
\operatorname{alg}^*(\mathcal T_N^{\mathrm{sym}})
=
\left(\bigl(\mathcal T_N^{\mathrm{sym}}\bigr)'\right)'
=
\{I,J\}'.
\]
The commutant of \(J\) consists exactly of matrices preserving the even and odd reflection subspaces. Therefore
\[
\operatorname{alg}^*(\mathcal T_N^{\mathrm{sym}})
=
\{J\}'
\cong
M_{N_+}(\mathbb C)\oplus M_{N_-}(\mathbb C),
\]
where
\[
N_+=\left\lceil\frac N2\right\rceil,
\qquad
N_-=\left\lfloor\frac N2\right\rfloor.
\]

Combining this with the Gaussian span theorem yields the following conclusion for the continuous paired-translation family on an equally spaced packet grid:
\[
\{T_s:s\in I\}'
=
\operatorname{span}_{\mathbb C}\{I,J\},
\]
for every interval \(I\subseteq\mathbb R\) with nonempty interior, and
\[
\operatorname{alg}^*\{T_s:s\in I\}
=
\{J\}'.
\]
Thus reflection is the only exact universal symmetry, and the reflection-even and reflection-odd subspaces are the only irreducible sectors of the generated finite operator algebra.
